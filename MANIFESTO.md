# The CurationsX Manifesto

Concise principles for Human × AI work. We hold ourselves — and every artifact in this repository — to these.

## 1. Humans own consequential decisions

AI drafts, suggests, and accelerates. A named human decides, and is accountable for the decision. No workflow here automates away that accountability.

## 2. Consent

People whose data, words, or likeness feed a Human × AI workflow should know and agree. Don't process what you weren't given.

## 3. Privacy by default

Minimize what you share with any model or tool. Strip identifiers before prompting. Assume anything you send may be retained; verify before sending anything you can't afford to have retained.

## 4. Accessibility

Artifacts should be usable by people with different abilities and tools: semantic structure, plain language, text equivalents for diagrams, no information locked in images.

## 5. Provenance

Every artifact identifies its human and AI contributions. Outputs carry enough trail — sources, versions, reviewers — that someone else can trace how they came to be.

## 6. Disclosure

When AI materially shaped work you share, say so. Quietly passing off AI output as unassisted human work erodes the trust this whole practice depends on.

## 7. Evaluation over vibes

"It looks good" is not verification. Define what good means before you run the work, then check against it. Our [quality rubric](docs/QUALITY.md) exists so judgments are inspectable.

## 8. Reversibility

Prefer actions you can undo. Every workflow specifies rollback and recovery before it specifies speed. Ambition is safe when retreat is cheap.

## 9. Graceful failure

Workflows plan for the AI being wrong, the tool being down, and the human being tired. Failure modes are documented, not discovered.

## 10. Nuance over automation

Surface uncertainty, ambiguity, and judgment points instead of hiding them behind confident output. A workflow that flags "a human should look at this" is more valuable than one that never admits doubt.
